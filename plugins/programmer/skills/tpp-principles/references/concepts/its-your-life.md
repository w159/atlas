# It's Your Life

**In brief:** You own your career and your working conditions, so you have the power and the responsibility to change what you don't like.

**Category:** Mindset
**Source:** Chapter 1, Topic 1 "It's Your Life"
**Also known as:** You Have Agency

## What it is
This is the opening idea of the book: your career belongs to you. You own it, you run it, you create it. The authors observe that many developers are frustrated, feeling they are stagnating, underpaid, underappreciated, or stuck on a toxic team, and their answer is always the same: "Why can't you change it?"

Software development sits near the top of any list of careers where you have real control. Skills are in demand, knowledge crosses geographic boundaries, remote work is possible, and pay is good. Despite this, developers often resist change. They hunker down and hope things improve, watch their skills go stale, and complain that their company won't train them.

The counter to this passivity is agency. If your environment is bad, try to fix it, but do not try forever. Martin Fowler's line captures the choice: "you can change your organization or change your organization." Either you improve where you are, or you move on.

## Why it matters
Waiting passively for conditions to improve lets your skills become dated and your options narrow. Taking ownership means you invest in yourself, ask for what you want, and act on the opportunities the industry offers. The cost of ignoring this is a career that happens to you instead of one you direct.

## In practice
- If your job is boring or your environment is bad, try to fix it, but set a limit on how long you try.
- If technology is passing you by, make time on your own clock to study what interests you. You are investing in yourself, so doing it off the clock is reasonable.
- Want to work remotely? Ask. If they say no, find someone who says yes.
- Be proactive and take the opportunities this industry gives you.
