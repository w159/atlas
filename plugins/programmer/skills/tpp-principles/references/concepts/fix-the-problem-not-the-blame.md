# Fix the Problem, Not the Blame

**In brief:** When a bug appears, spend your energy fixing it rather than assigning fault, because it is your problem either way.

**Category:** Mindset
**Source:** Chapter 3, Topic 20 "Debugging"
**Also known as:** Fix the Problem Not the Blame

## What it is
Debugging is a sensitive, emotional subject, and one common failure mode is turning it into a hunt for a culprit. Having found someone else's bug, you can pour time and energy into laying blame on the "filthy culprit" who wrote it. In some workplaces that is part of the culture and may even feel cathartic.

The book rejects this for the technical arena. It does not matter whether the bug is your fault or someone else's; it is still your problem. So concentrate on fixing the problem, not fixing the blame.

## Why it matters
Blame is wasted effort. It does nothing to move the defect toward resolution, it poisons team relationships, and it feeds the denial and finger-pointing that make debugging harder. Treating every bug as your problem to solve keeps attention on the puzzle and out of the politics.

## In practice
Adopt the stance that any bug in front of you is yours to resolve regardless of origin. Pair this with the wider debugging mindset: stay calm, do not panic, and chase the root cause. (Raising a bug that stemmed from a wrong assumption with the whole team is separate guidance, given under Tip 34, "Don't Assume It - Prove It" - see [dont-assume-prove-it](dont-assume-prove-it.md).)
