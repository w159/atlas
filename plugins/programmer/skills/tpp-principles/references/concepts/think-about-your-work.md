# Think! About Your Work

**In brief:** Think about what you are doing while you are doing it: an ongoing critical appraisal of every decision you make, every day, on every project.

**Category:** Principle
**Source:** From the Preface to the First Edition

## What it is
The second of the two most basic characteristics all Pragmatic Programmers share, stated as Tip 2. The authors challenge you to think about what you are doing while you are doing it. This is not a one-time audit of current practices; it is an ongoing critical appraisal of every decision you make, every day, and on every project. Never run on auto-pilot. Constantly be thinking, critiquing your work in real time. The old IBM corporate motto, THINK!, is the Pragmatic Programmer's mantra.

## Why it matters
The authors concede this sounds like hard work and will take up time that is probably already under tremendous pressure. The reward is a more active involvement with a job you love, a feeling of mastery over an increasing range of subjects, and pleasure in a feeling of continuous improvement. Over the long term the time investment is repaid as you and your team become more efficient, write code that is easier to maintain, and spend less time in meetings.

## In practice
- Never run on auto-pilot; critique your work in real time.
- Do not take things as given without first getting the facts. When colleagues say "because that's the way it's done," or a vendor promises the solution to all your problems, smell a challenge (the "critical thinker" characteristic).
- Adjust your approach to suit the current circumstances and environment, and do it continuously as the work progresses.
- Judge the relative importance of all the factors affecting a project and use your experience to produce appropriate solutions.
