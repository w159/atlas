# Boiled Frog

**In brief:** Watch the big picture, because slow, incremental change is easy to not notice until the accumulated result has cooked you.

**Category:** Mindset
**Source:** Chapter 1, Topic 4 "Stone Soup and Boiled Frogs"
**Also known as:** Remember the Big Picture, Don't Be the Boiled Frog

## What it is
The fable says that a frog dropped into boiling water jumps straight back out, but a frog placed in cold water that is heated gradually will not notice the slow rise in temperature and will stay put until it is cooked. The authors admit they have never tried it, but the lesson stands.

This is the other side of the stone soup story. Where the soldiers are catalysts, the villagers focus so tightly on the stones that they forget about the rest of the world. We all fall for this every day. Projects slowly and inexorably get out of hand. Most software disasters start too small to notice, and most project overruns happen one day at a time. Systems drift from their specifications feature by feature, and patch after patch accumulates until nothing of the original is left. It is the accumulation of small things that breaks morale and teams.

The frog's problem is distinct from the broken windows problem. With broken windows, people lose the will to fight entropy because they perceive that no one else cares. The frog simply does not notice the change.

## Why it matters
Gradual drift is dangerous precisely because no alarm goes off. Each individual change looks harmless, so nothing prompts a reaction, and by the time the cumulative effect is obvious the situation is already dire. Keeping the big picture in view is what lets you catch the slow boil early enough to jump out.

## In practice
- Don't be like the fabled frog. Keep an eye on the big picture.
- Constantly review what is happening around you, not just what you personally are doing.
- Practice situational awareness: notice your surroundings deliberately, then do the same for your project. Ask what looks out of context or like it does not belong.
- When catalyzing change, check whether you are making stone soup (everyone benefits) or frog soup (gradual deception that harms).
