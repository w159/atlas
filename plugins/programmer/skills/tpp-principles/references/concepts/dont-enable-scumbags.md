# Don't Enable Scumbags

**In brief:** If a project skirts the bounds of ethical behavior, working on it makes you just as responsible as its sponsors.

**Category:** Principle
**Source:** Postface

## What it is
The second of the two questions the authors say we have a duty to ask about every piece of code we deliver: "Would I use this myself?" They describe it as a judgment related to the Golden Rule: would I be happy to be a user of this software? Do I want my details shared? Do I want my movements to be given to retail outlets? Would I be happy to be driven by this autonomous vehicle? Am I comfortable doing this?

Some inventive ideas begin to skirt the bounds of ethical behavior, and if you are involved in that project, you are just as responsible as the sponsors. No matter how many degrees of separation you might rationalize, that rule remains true.

## Why it matters
Developers are, in the authors' words, incredibly privileged: we are truly building the future, and with that extraordinary power comes an extraordinary responsibility. The postface closes by putting the outcome in your hands: recognize when you are doing something against the future you want to inhabit, and have the courage to say "no!" Envision the future we could have, and have the courage to create it.

## In practice
- Before shipping, ask the Golden Rule question: would I be happy to be a user of this software?
- Do not rationalize your distance from an unethical project; degrees of separation do not remove your responsibility.
- When a project works against a future you would want to inhabit, say no.
